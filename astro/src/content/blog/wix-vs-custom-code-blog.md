## First, what Wix actually gives you

Wix works. I'm not going to pretend it doesn't. You can sign up, drag things around, and have something that looks like a website in an afternoon.

But here's what you're actually signing up for:

- **Their subdomain** (yourbusiness.wixsite.com) until you pay for a domain
- **Their branding** in your footer — even on paid plans
- **Their servers** — which you share with thousands of other sites
- **Their templates** — which your competitor three towns over is probably also using
- **Their rules** — if they change pricing, kill a feature, or shut down your plan, you have no exit

You don't own anything. You're renting a shop inside someone else's mall.

## What actually hurts you on Google

This is the part nobody talks about honestly.

Wix has improved over the years — Google can crawl it now, yes. But "Google can crawl it" is not the same as "Google will rank it."

Here's what Wix still gets wrong for most Indian SMEs:

**Page speed.** Wix loads a lot of JavaScript by default — animations, their editor logic, tracking scripts. On a mobile connection in Palampur or Pathankot, that adds up. Google's Core Web Vitals score suffers. Your ranking suffers with it.

**Code bloat.** Every Wix page ships HTML and CSS for features you never turned on. A custom site ships only what's on the page.

**Schema markup.** If you're a tour operator, a clinic, or a local business — Google rewards structured data (business name, hours, reviews, service areas). Wix gives you limited control over this. A custom build lets me wire in JSON-LD schema that directly tells Google what your business is and where it operates.

**llms.txt.** This one is newer. AI search engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude — now crawl websites differently from Google. A file called `llms.txt` tells them exactly what your site is about. Wix doesn't support this. Every custom site I ship has one.

One of my clients, InHimalayas, saw a sudden surge in AI-referred traffic within weeks of launch — because the site was built for AI crawlers from day one. That's not possible on Wix.

## When Wix makes sense

I'll be straight: Wix is fine if —

- You're testing a business idea and need something up in 24 hours
- You'll be updating content yourself every day and can't wait on a developer
- You genuinely have no budget and need literally anything

But if you're a real business — a tour operator, a clinic, a school, a D2C brand — and you want Google to find you, customers to trust you, and leads to actually reach you: Wix is a ceiling, not a launchpad.
