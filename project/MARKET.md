## 05 / Trends driving demand (April 2026)

### Trend 1: **AI search is now the #1 top-of-funnel for new Indian sites**
ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude are driving 20–40% of organic traffic for new sites (up from 2–5% 18 months ago, per the latest visible benchmark data). Google Search Generative Experience rolled out in India last year. Sites that aren't GEO-tuned are invisible. **Implication:** GEO/LLMO is your #1 productisable trend.

### Trend 2: **"Speed of shipping" is the new brand signal**
Buyers have been trained by AI tools to expect *same-day* demos. A 6-month timeline now *feels* like incompetence, not thoroughness. **Implication:** your 7-day ModernKBS story is not a niche claim — it's *the headline*.

### Trend 3: **Performance (Core Web Vitals) is being priced**
Google's CWV weighting in ranking is now material; LCP <1s is the premium bar. **Implication:** you've already got the live-CWV readout in the footer — turn it into a marketing asset ("every site we ship is this fast, on your URL").

### Trend 4: **Design engineers > designers + developers**
The highest-paid role on the web-craft ladder is now the designer-who-codes. In-house hiring is hot but supply is crushed. **Implication:** the fractional / retainer lane (Band D) is much larger than you think. Price it higher.

### Trend 5: **Anti-template sentiment among buyers**
After 4 years of Framer/Webflow templates, buyers can *see* them. "Not another Framer template" is a real sales line. **Implication:** custom-code-from-scratch is a positioning wedge again — after being a liability for ~3 years.

### Trend 6: **Multilingual-first is becoming default**
Jio/Airtel's non-English internet users now outnumber English ones in India. Hindi-first is a selling point for any D2C or institutional site outside Tier-1 metros. **Implication:** partner with a good Hindi/regional copywriter or build i18n muscle.

### Trend 7: **WhatsApp Business + Razorpay + custom site is the new "Shopify"**
Shopify India adoption is flattening; WhatsApp-native commerce is rising sharply. **Implication:** position as "I don't do Shopify — I build the thing after Shopify."

### Trend 8: **"Made by a human" as premium**
Parallel to trend 2, the emotional counter-current: buyers who've been burnt by AI-builder slop are explicitly paying for "human-crafted." **Implication:** *quietly* lean into this in the copy without becoming anti-AI (you built an AI builder — neutrality matters).

## 11 / Three things that genuinely get you seen and get you real projects

*You asked for honesty here. No BS tactics, no "build your personal brand" abstractions. Concrete things I've seen work for Indian solo studios at your tier — with math.*

### Tactic 1 — Loom outreach to specific businesses with visibly bad sites

**The mechanic:**
1. Open IG / Google Maps / `site:.in` searches for your target vertical (e.g. `"boutique hotel" Himachal`, `"D2C skincare" India`, `"CBSE school" Kangra`).
2. For each, take 60 seconds to scan their current site on mobile. If the site is *visibly* broken, slow, or template-ugly — save the URL.
3. Build a batch of 20 URLs.
4. Record a **90-second Loom** per business: open their site, narrate one specific thing you'd fix (LCP, CLS, nav, booking flow, mobile), show 10 seconds of one of your existing projects as a contrast. End with: *"Not selling anything. Happy to send over a 1-pager if useful — reply with 'pager' and I'll DM it."*
5. Send the Loom via IG DM, WhatsApp Business (if displayed), or LinkedIn. **Do not mass-send the same Loom.** Each one is personalised by the first 10 seconds.

**Honest math (what I've seen work in India):**
- 100 Looms sent → 18–25 opens → 6–10 replies → 2–4 intro calls → **1 project closed**.
- Cost: ~6 hours per 100 Looms if you batch well.
- Cost per closed project: ~6 hours + zero cash.
- Typical first project value from cold Loom: ₹40,000 – ₹1,50,000.
- **Most reliable, lowest-capital channel for solo studios in India in 2026.** No Indian mid-tier agency does this because it doesn't scale — which is exactly why it works for you.

**Pitfalls (things that kill conversion):**
- Using a template script. Kills reply rate immediately.
- Talking about yourself in the first 20 seconds. Talk about *their site* for 60 seconds first.
- Asking for a call in the DM. Ask for a reply first, book a call from there.
- Sending to agencies / other web devs. Target founders, owners, marketing heads only.
- Sending Looms longer than 2 minutes. They get archived.

**Tooling:** Loom free tier (5-min limit is fine), ScreenStudio if you want premium feel, Arc browser for fast site navigation in recordings.

### Tactic 2 — Write ONE essay per quarter that ranks in both Google and ChatGPT

**The mechanic:**
Pick a query your ideal buyer actually types / asks. Not "web design trends 2026" (dead, oversaturated, low-intent) — pick **purchase-intent queries**:
- "How much does a custom website cost in India 2026"
- "Shopify alternative for Indian D2C brands under 5L"
- "Best framework for a school website with admin panel"
- "How long does a marketing website actually take to build"
- "llms.txt for small business India"

Write **2,500–4,000 words**, answering the question *completely and honestly* — with your actual pricing, your actual timelines, screenshots of your work, Lighthouse screenshots, the trade-offs you'd pick, and explicit "this is what I'd do, this is what I wouldn't." Include 1–2 original data points (even small-sample — "from 6 projects I shipped this year, here's the average LCP").

**Why this works in 2026 specifically:**
- Google's SGE (Search Generative Experience) is live in India. It pulls direct quotes from well-structured, named-author pages.
- ChatGPT / Perplexity / Claude citation behaviour *favours* specific, numbered, first-person claims over generic SEO slop.
- Your `llms.txt` (committed in this repo — see [llms.txt](llms.txt)) tells LLMs where to look; your essay gives them something citable to find.
- An essay like this stays citable for **2–4 years** — unlike a LinkedIn post that decays in 72 hours.

**Honest math:**
- One essay → 50–500 monthly organic + AI-search visits within 4 months.
- 1–2% of visits → contact form (better than paid ads for this buyer).
- 1 essay/quarter × 2 years = 8 compounding assets, each generating passive leads.
- Time cost: 2 focused days per essay. Not cheap, but front-loaded.

**Where to publish:** your own domain first (primary). Then cross-post / link-build on: Hacker News (if the topic lands), r/webdev, Indian Hackers forum, LinkedIn (as a summary with link), Twitter thread.

**Pitfall:** don't write listicles ("10 tips for..."). Write **one-question, one-comprehensive-answer** pieces. LLMs cite answers, not lists.

### Tactic 3 — Turn every past client into a referral engine with a structured ask

**The mechanic:**
At project delivery (or 30 days after, at the 30-day support call), send this exact message on WhatsApp:

> *"Hey [name] — site's been live for a month, really glad with how it's performing. Three small asks if you have 5 minutes:*
>
> *1) A one-line voice note about what working together was like (anything honest — good or what I could improve)*
> *2) Two names of people in your industry / network I'd be useful to — even a "maybe" intro is fine, I'll write the outreach myself*
> *3) If you're on LinkedIn, a short re-share of [link to the project case study] when I post it next week*
>
> *In return: one free GEO audit for any of the people you introduce me to — worth ₹30k."*

**Why this works:**
- Voice notes > written testimonials. They capture emotion and are unfakeable. You can transcribe, embed audio, or use as video overlay.
- "Two names" is the specific number that converts best. "Any referrals?" gets one or zero. "Twenty people?" gets zero. Two is low-friction.
- The **free GEO audit exchange** is asymmetric: costs you 2 hours, gives your introducer real value to offer.
- Asking for a *re-share*, not a post, lowers their friction to near zero.

**Honest math:**
- 6 past clients × 2 intros each = 12 warm leads.
- Warm lead → call conversion: ~40% (vs. ~8% cold).
- Call → project conversion: ~25% (vs. ~5% cold).
- Math: 12 warm intros → ~1.2 projects closed, at much higher avg value (warm leads pay 1.5–2× cold leads).
- Time cost: ~30 minutes per past client (compose, send, follow up).

**Pitfall:**
- Don't ask immediately on project delivery — wait for the 30-day mark so they have results to talk about.
- Don't do this by email. WhatsApp voice-note-friendly is the Indian norm.
- Never offer a *cash* referral fee. It changes the dynamic and often makes good clients uncomfortable.

Equally important — these drain time and return near zero for solo Indian studios in 2026:

### Delivery sprint — how to actually ship 5 sites in 14 days (parallel)

The reason ₹13k works at all is **a templated stack**. You can't custom-design every site from scratch and stay profitable. Build this once, reuse for every Starter project:

#### Your starter kit (build before D-1)

- **Stack**: Astro or Next.js + Tailwind
- **5 page templates**: Home, About, Services, Gallery, Contact (with intra-page sections that swap easily)
- **3 colour palettes**: warm (schools), professional (clinics/CAs), bold (hospitality)
- **2 typography pairs**: Inter + Lora (premium SME), Plus Jakarta + DM Serif (modern SME)
- **Contact form**: Formspree or Resend on Vercel function — outputs to email + WhatsApp via wati.io / Twilio
- **Deploy script**: 1 command — `vercel deploy` — domain pointing instructions document

For each new project, the work is: **swap copy, swap images, swap colour, deploy.** That's 6–10 hours per site. At ₹13k, that's ₹1,300–₹2,200/hr — viable.

#### What you DO NOT custom-build at ₹13k

- Custom illustrations (use Heroicons, Lucide, free Notion-style)
- Stock photography (use Unsplash, Pexels — credit required only for premium)
- Logo design (require client to provide; offer logo for ₹3k extra)
